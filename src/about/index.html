{{title}}

Mac was born in South Haven in the early 1980s and grew up in Jackson in the 1990s. Graduating in 2003 from Michigan State University's James Madison College with dual degrees in international relations and Russian language and literature, Mac worked in the financial services sector in New York for nearly a decade before moving to Detroit in 2012.

Starting in a volunteer position on Mike Duggan's initial campaign for mayor in 2013, Mac worked for the Mayor and the City for nearly three years, starting as a phone bank volunteer, finishing the campaign as finance director, and working through Detroit's Chapter 13 municipal bankruptcy in the area of operational restructuring. From working on projects related to income taxes to EMS response time to public lighting to infrastructure.

Having lived on the east side since 2014, Mac has been a member and leader of the Indian Village Association (former board member), Pingree Park Association (founding President), Indian Village Tennis Club (current board member), employed by the Villages Community Development Corporation (current executive director), Jefferson Avenue Presbyterian Church (current member) and the Detroit Yacht Club (current member). His work in advocacy has directly led to the deployment of speed humps throughout Detroit, the clearing out of hundreds of alleys, and getting many different pieces of infrastructure fixed (sidewalks, trees, utility issues, etc.).

By continuing to focus on outcomes and facts, Mac believes that we can continue to build a truly united and prosperous Detroit where differences of opinion are cause for collaboration, not recrimination.
